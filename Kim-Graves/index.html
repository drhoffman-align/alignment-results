Your Personalized Recommendations

Your scores reveal someone who has done meaningful work processing your past and has a clear sense of where you're headed, but you're experiencing some friction in the present moment. With your past at 80% and future at 74%, you've built a solid foundation and maintain strong vision — yet your present sits at 68%, suggesting you're navigating some tension between where you've been and where you're going. This pattern often emerges when someone has grown significantly but is still calibrating how to live from that new place.

Your lowest areas — habits and energy in the present, and goals and ambition for the future — are actually your greatest leverage points. Research consistently shows that sustainable change happens through small, consistent actions rather than dramatic overhauls, which makes your habits and energy score particularly important. When our daily rhythms aren't supporting us, even clear vision can feel exhausting to pursue. Similarly, when goals feel unclear or overwhelming, it creates a subtle but persistent drain on motivation that affects everything else.

Since you've done solid foundational work but are experiencing this performance gap between clarity and consistent action, working with a coach could help you translate your insights into sustainable daily practices. The goal would be building systems that make your vision feel more accessible and less effortful to pursue.

  • Track your energy patterns for one week — note what time of day you feel most focused and schedule your most important work during those windows
  • Choose one existing habit and attach a new 2-minute health practice to it (like doing 10 pushups after brushing your teeth)
  • Write down your top 3 goals and identify the smallest possible next step for each — something you could complete in under 30 minutes
  • Set a weekly 15-minute appointment with yourself to review what's working in your routines and what needs adjustment
  • Create a simple end-of-day ritual that helps you transition from work mode to personal time, even if it's just 5 minutes of intentional breathing
