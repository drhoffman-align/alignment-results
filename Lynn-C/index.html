Your Personalized Recommendations

Your scores reveal someone who has done meaningful work understanding your past and carries strong clarity about your core identity and values, yet you're experiencing tension in translating that foundation into present-moment awareness and future security. You're operating from a place of genuine growth orientation and resilience, but there's a gap between your self-knowledge and your day-to-day emotional navigation, particularly around financial planning and creating the concrete structures that support your deeper sense of purpose.

Your lowest areas—self-awareness in the present moment, financial intentionality, and meaning contribution—aren't weaknesses but rather the precise places where focused attention will unlock the most momentum. Research consistently shows that emotional intelligence and financial security are foundational to psychological well-being, not because money itself creates happiness, but because financial clarity reduces cognitive load and frees mental energy for the relationships and contributions that matter most to you. When we can't track our emotional patterns in real-time or feel uncertain about our financial foundation, it becomes much harder to show up fully in our work and relationships.

Given that several core areas are feeling heavy right now, it might be worth exploring whether your brain needs some additional support to function the way it's meant to. A psychiatrist understands these patterns from a neurobiological perspective, and the most effective approach is usually an ongoing conversation rather than just a prescription. This kind of foundational support often makes other growth work more accessible and sustainable.

  • Set a daily 5-minute timer to check in with your emotional state—name what you're feeling and rate its intensity from 1-10, then note what triggered it
  • Schedule one 2-hour block this weekend to list all your accounts, debts, and monthly expenses in a simple spreadsheet
  • Choose one small way to contribute meaningfully each week—mentor a newer nurse, write one encouraging note to a colleague, or volunteer 30 minutes for a cause you care about
  • Create a simple evening routine where you write down three specific things you accomplished that day and how they connected to your larger values
  • Set up automatic transfers of even $25-50 per month into a separate savings account to begin building financial momentum

Recommended Resources

Books

  • The Gifts of Imperfection by Brené Brown — This book directly addresses building self-awareness and emotional intelligence by helping you recognize your patterns and develop the courage to be authentic with yourself.
  • Get Good with Money by Tiffany Aliche — Written by an African American financial educator, this book provides a clear, step-by-step system for building financial security without overwhelm, perfect for creating the visibility and structure you need.

Practices

  • Emotion Labeling — When you feel a strong emotion, pause and simply name it out loud: "I'm feeling frustrated" or "That triggered anxiety" before taking any action.
  • Weekly Money Check-In — Set a 15-minute weekly appointment with yourself to review where your money went that week, building the financial awareness that reduces anxiety.

Tools

  • Copilot Money — A comprehensive personal finance tracker that automatically categorizes spending and tracks investments, available at copilot.money for iOS and Mac users.

Professional Support

  • Therapy for Self-Awareness and Emotional Patterns — A therapist who specializes in emotional regulation and self-awareness can help you understand why you react before you reflect and give you practical tools to build that pause between feeling and acting.
