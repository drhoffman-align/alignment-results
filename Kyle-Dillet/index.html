Your Personalized Recommendations

You've done meaningful work to understand yourself and move past what's held you back — that 80% in your past shows real resolution. Right now, you're managing the present with skill, balancing competing demands and staying grounded even when things feel tight. But there's a gap between where you are and where you're aiming: your future vision isn't pulling you forward with the same clarity or energy. This isn't about lacking capability — it's about translating what you know into a direction that feels both compelling and concrete.

The areas asking for your attention — work and purpose, goals and ambition, financial intentionality — are all forward-facing. They're not about fixing something broken; they're about building the architecture for what comes next. Research on goal-setting shows that vague aspirations drain motivation, while specific, structured plans activate the brain's reward systems and create momentum. Right now, your future feels more like a concept than a plan, and that's exactly why it's harder to move toward it with confidence.

Given where you are, working with a coach could be valuable — not because something's wrong, but because you're ready to turn clarity into consistent action. A coach helps close the gap between knowing what matters and actually building it into your life. That said, the real work is tactical: you need to get specific about what you're building and why it matters enough to prioritize.

  • Write down three professional outcomes you want within the next 18 months — not aspirations, but measurable results. Then identify the single highest-leverage action for each and schedule it in the next two weeks.
  • Block 90 minutes this week to map your financial picture: current state, three-month runway, and one specific money decision you've been avoiding. Make that decision by the end of the session.
  • Choose one area of your consulting work that feels misaligned or draining. Write a one-page plan to either delegate it, restructure it, or exit it within 60 days.
  • Set a weekly 30-minute appointment with yourself — same day, same time — to review what moved forward and what didn't. Treat it like a client meeting.
  • Identify one person in your field who's built something you respect. Reach out for a 20-minute conversation about how they made key decisions during a similar transition.

Recommended Resources

Books

  • The 12 Week Year by Brian Moran — This book will help you close the gap between vision and execution by breaking down annual goals into focused 12-week sprints that create immediate momentum and accountability.
  • Essentialism by Greg McKeown — Perfect for a consultant feeling drained by misaligned work, this teaches you how to identify what truly matters and eliminate the rest so your energy flows toward purpose instead of obligation.
  • Set Boundaries, Find Peace by Nedra Glover Tawwab — Since self-acceptance sits at your lowest past score, this will help you understand how saying yes to the wrong things keeps you from honoring what you actually need to feel whole.

Practices

  • Weekly Purpose Audit — Every Friday, write down three moments from the week when you felt energized versus three that felt draining, then look for patterns about what type of work or clients actually align with you.
  • The One Goal Morning — Each day, before opening email or Slack, identify the single most important task that moves you toward your defined goal and complete it first, building momentum through daily wins rather than waiting for perfect clarity.

Tools
