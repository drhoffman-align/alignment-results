Your Personalized Recommendations

Your scores reveal someone who has built a solid foundation from your past experiences but is currently struggling to translate that wisdom into forward momentum. You have strong clarity about your work and purpose, and you've developed good self-awareness about who you are at your core. However, you're finding it challenging to channel that foundation into active learning and concrete goals for the future. This creates a pattern where you feel grounded in what you've accomplished but uncertain about where you're headed next.

Your lowest areas — learning orientation and goal-setting — are actually where the greatest opportunity lies. Research shows that when we stop actively learning and pursuing specific objectives, we often experience what psychologists call "drift," where days feel busy but lack direction. These aren't personal shortcomings; they're simply the areas where focused attention will create the most significant shift in how purposeful and energized you feel day-to-day.

Given that multiple core areas are feeling heavy right now, it might be worth exploring whether your brain has the support it needs to function the way it's meant to. A psychiatrist understands these patterns specifically and approaches it as an ongoing conversation about what's happening, not just prescribing solutions. This kind of support often makes everything else feel more manageable.

  • Choose one specific skill or topic that genuinely interests you and commit to spending 20 minutes learning about it three times this week — whether through a podcast, article, or short video
  • Write down one concrete goal you want to accomplish in the next 30 days, break it into three smaller steps, and schedule the first step in your calendar for this weekend
  • Set up a simple habit tracker for one health-related behavior (like drinking water, taking a walk, or going to bed by a certain time) and check it off daily for two weeks
  • Schedule 15 minutes each Sunday to review what you learned that week and identify one thing you want to explore or accomplish in the coming week
  • Find one person in your life who shares an interest or goal similar to yours and suggest meeting or talking regularly about your progress

Recommended Resources

Books

  • The 12 Week Year by Brian Moran — This system transforms overwhelming long-term goals into manageable 12-week sprints, giving you the momentum and clarity you need to bridge the gap between vision and action.
  • Get Good with Money by Tiffany Aliche — A practical, step-by-step approach to building financial security that breaks down money management into achievable milestones rather than overwhelming concepts.
  • Atomic Habits by James Clear — Shows you how to build the small, consistent actions that create lasting change, especially when motivation feels low and growth feels uncomfortable.

Practices

  • Weekly Goal Setting — Every Sunday, choose one specific, measurable goal you can complete in the next seven days, then track your progress daily to build momentum through completion.
  • Growth Comfort Zone Expansion — Each week, deliberately choose one small area where you'll be uncomfortable in service of learning something new, treating the discomfort as data rather than danger.

Tools

  • Copilot Money — Tracks spending, budgets, investments, and net worth automatically across all your accounts, available at copilot.money and through the App Store.
  • Todoist — A task management app that helps you break down big goals into actionable steps with due dates and progress tracking, available at todoist.com.
