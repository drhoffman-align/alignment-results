Your Personalized Recommendations

Your scores reveal someone who has done remarkable work processing your past — you've achieved an extraordinary level of self-acceptance and clarity about who you are. You're navigating the present with solid awareness, though it requires constant attention and energy. The challenge lies in your relationship with the future, where uncertainty about goals and financial direction is creating significant stress and making it difficult to move forward with confidence.

Your lowest scoring areas — goals, financial security, and meaning — aren't weaknesses to fix, but rather the precise places where focused attention will unlock the most momentum. Research shows that when we lack clear direction, our brains default to threat-detection mode, which explains why the future feels overwhelming despite your strong foundation. These areas matter because they're the bridge between your hard-won self-knowledge and actually building the life you want.

Given how much foundational work you've already done, this feels like a performance gap rather than something deeper. A coach could help you translate all that self-awareness into concrete action and consistent progress. The clarity is there — it's the execution system that needs attention.

  • Set aside 20 minutes this week to write down three specific things you want to accomplish in the next 90 days, then break each one into the smallest possible first step
  • Track your spending for two weeks without changing anything — just observe where your money goes to create awareness before making any changes
  • Schedule one 15-minute conversation with someone whose career or financial approach you respect, asking them one specific question about how they made a particular decision
  • Each evening, write down one thing that felt meaningful about your day, no matter how small — this builds the neural pathway between daily actions and larger purpose
  • Choose one financial goal (even something small like saving $200) and set up an automatic transfer to make it happen without relying on willpower

Recommended Resources

Books

  • Get Good with Money by Tiffany Aliche — This book provides a clear, step-by-step system for building financial security from the ground up, perfect for transforming financial overwhelm into actionable clarity.
  • The 12 Week Year by Brian Moran — This approach breaks down ambitious goals into manageable 12-week cycles, helping you build momentum when long-term planning feels overwhelming.

Practices

  • Daily Financial Check-In — Spend 5 minutes each morning reviewing yesterday's spending and today's financial priorities to build awareness without judgment.
  • Three Good Things — Each evening, write down three things that went well or didn't go wrong that day to retrain your brain to notice stability alongside problems.

Tools

  • Copilot Money — A comprehensive money tracking app that automatically categorizes spending and tracks investments, available at copilot.money and on iOS/Android app stores.
  • YNAB (You Need A Budget) — A budgeting system that helps you assign every dollar a job before you spend it, creating the financial structure that reduces anxiety through clarity.

Professional Support

  • Financial Therapy for Money Anxiety — A therapist who specializes in financial psychology can help you understand the emotional patterns behind money avoidance and build healthier financial relationships. Find therapists at psychologytoday.com/us/therapists or explore affordable options at openpathcollective.org.
