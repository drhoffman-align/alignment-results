Frequently Asked Questions

"I scored 81%. Why does the 2.75 in Habits matter if everything else is strong?"

Because your strengths don't exist in isolation — they run on a biological substrate. Your identity clarity, relational authenticity, and future vision are all processed by a brain that runs on sleep, nutrition, and physical recovery.

The 2.75 doesn't negate the 81%. But it tells you where your energy is quietly leaking. Addressing it doesn't mean you're broken — it means you're smart enough to protect what you've built.

"How do I build a habit when I'm already at capacity?"

You don't add a habit. You anchor one. Anchoring means choosing one thing that happens before everything else competes for the space — the first 10 minutes of morning, the 5 minutes before dinner, the moment you close your laptop.

Start with one behavior so small that not doing it would feel like a choice, not a failure. When that's stable, you can add.

"Should I be further along than 77% in the present after 28 years?"

No — and that framing is worth examining. 77% is not "behind." For someone carrying the emotional weight of senior DEI leadership in a public institution, 77% in the present is evidence of extraordinary regulation, not failure.

The more useful question: What's keeping me here, and is it within my control to change? The answer is yes — the physical maintenance gap is directly addressable.

"How do I know if I need therapy vs. coaching for the self-forgiveness work?"

Coaching works with the present and future, building toward goals. Therapy works with patterns rooted in the past, especially when those patterns activate under stress in ways that feel disproportionate or hard to shift through insight alone.

If old material surfaces in ways that feel bigger than the present situation warrants, a therapist is the right tool. Many people at your level benefit from both.

"How often should I retake the Alignment Score?"

Every 90 days is the recommended rhythm. That's long enough for real behavioral change to show up in scores, but short enough to course-correct if you've drifted. Your next reassessment target: May 12, 2026.

"What if my scores get worse on the next assessment?"

That's useful information, not failure. It usually means either you're being more honest now than before (very common — self-awareness increases with reflection), or life has genuinely shifted. Either way, you have clearer data to work with. The score isn't the goal. The alignment is.
