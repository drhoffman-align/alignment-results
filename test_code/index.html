The area of temperament awareness deserves particular attention, as research in personality psychology shows that understanding your natural behavioral tendencies is foundational to emotional regulation and decision-making. Your Action Plan

Temperament Awareness WEEK 1: Set three alarms (10am, 2pm, 6pm). When they ring, log: energy 1–10, emotion word, last meal, last 90 min activity, and one body tension spot. Do 2 minutes of box breathing (4-4-4-4). If energy ≤4, take a 10-minute no-screen walk or stretch. Use your phone Notes or a printed sheet. WEEKS 2-4: Each Sunday, review your log and choose one high-energy and one low-energy window. For three weeks: schedule your top task in the high-energy window (30–60 min), put admin/errands in the low-energy window (15–30 min), and set a 5-minute reset every 90 minutes (stand, water, breathe). Add a “social recovery” rule: leave one event at the 60-minute mark. Track completion with a daily checkbox. MONTHS 2-3: Create a one-page Personal Operating Manual: best focus hours, ideal sleep/wake, food/hydration notes, break frequency, red/yellow/green day actions. Implement it: protect two 90-minute deep-work blocks weekly in best hours, prep your workspace the night before, and schedule one 3–4 hour rest block weekly. Do a 30-minute weekly audit to update the manual. Relationships & Authenticity WEEK 1: Pick one safe person. Send a message today to schedule a 30-minute call/coffee. Before it, write one honest sentence you’ll say (e.g., “I’ve been people-pleasing and want to practice being more direct”). During the chat, state one preference or boundary. After, jot three bullets: energy before/after, what felt scary, what felt easier. WEEKS 2-4: Do one “honesty rep” per week. Options: decline a small request without a justification, share a true preference, or ask for specific help. Use a two-sentence cap to avoid over-explaining. After each rep, record date, person, words you used, and outcome. Limit social commitments to two per week and put an exit time in your calendar. MONTHS 2-3: Build a circle of three. List three relationships and set recurring check-ins (biweekly, 30 minutes each). Use a vulnerability ladder: weeks 5–8 share small truths; weeks 9–12 share one medium truth (need, boundary, fear). Practice one boundary per month in a real scenario and log the result. Goals & Ambition WEEK 1: Choose one priority goal. Define a two-week deliverable (e.g., “Draft 3-page outline”). Set up a simple board (sticky notes/app) with To Do/Doing/Done. Block a daily 45-minute focus slot at the same time for four days. Each session: put phone in another room, start a timer, complete one task, move one sticky to Done, then spend 5 minutes setting up tomorrow’s first step. WEEKS 2-4: Run a weekly planning ritual (Sunday, 30 minutes). Break the goal into three outcomes per week and schedule them. Each weekday, set one MVT (most valuable task) and do it in a 60–90 minute block. Nightly, lay out tools, open the right doc, and enable a site blocker. Do a Friday 30-minute review to measure progress and adjust next week’s plan. MONTHS 2-3: Work in 2-week sprints. Sprint planning: pick 3–5 tasks with clear “done” definitions (demo, link, or email). Protect four deep-work blocks per week and add a mid-sprint buffer block. End each sprint by sharing a tangible checkpoint (send draft, post summary, or show demo). Keep a visible scoreboard and run a 30-minute retrospective to refine your system.
